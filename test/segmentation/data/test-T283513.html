Calpurnius Piso bore the same name as his father, Gnaeus Calpurnius Piso. The Calpurnii Pisones formed the main branch of the gens, and already counted 8 consuls by 23 BC. Piso married a daughter of a Marcus Popillius and they had at least two sons: Gnaeus Calpurnius Piso, who later became consul in 7 BC; and Lucius Calpurnius Piso, consul in 1 BC.[3]
